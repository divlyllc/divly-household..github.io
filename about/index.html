About Divly

We build for the household, not the individual

Divly's mission is to help the two people running a home run it as a team: everything out of your heads, everything owned, and the heavy planning done for you. So you can stop managing your life together and start living it.

Less managing. More living.

Built by the household that needed it

Think about the last weekend you were fully present. Not mentally rehearsing Monday, not half-tracking what you forgot to schedule, not carrying a quiet list in the back of your head. Just there. That feeling is what Divly is built to give back.

We're a two-person founding team: an engineer and a product manager. At work, nothing we ship goes unowned. At home, the dentist appointment, the empty fridge and the weekend plan lived wherever they landed, which was usually one of our heads. It mostly worked, until it really didn't.

Then we brought home our first child. Waking every two hours for feedings, we couldn't remember if the dog had been fed. Things as small as brushing your teeth got lost in the fog of those first weeks. The system we didn't have stopped being a quirk and became the problem, and we learned the hard way that nobody can intentionally share what nobody can see. When the work of running a home is invisible, it gets divided by default instead of by design, and a newborn is the stress test that default fails.

We tried every app and ended up running several at once. Nothing showed us everything at a glance, so what actually needed doing got buried in one of many shared lists. Simple tasks couldn't just surface for the day, habits had no home at all, and lists stopped meaning anything when they had to hold everything. We needed all three, tasks, habits and lists, to be the best versions of ourselves, and no app gave our household all three.

So we built the app we kept looking for. Tasks that surface when they matter. Habits that keep the small daily things from slipping. Lists reserved for when a list actually makes sense. All in one view, with one rule we refuse to bend: nothing exists in Divly without an owner.

We run our own home on Divly every day. Every feature you see survived the hardest test we know: a real household, a real week, and two real people who would rather be living than managing.

What we believe

Both of you, by design. Divly only works when both people are in it. Not one organizer inviting a reluctant guest: two adults running the show, with the same complete picture.

Ownership over sharing. Shared lists create ambiguity. Ownership creates trust. Every task in Divly has one owner, from the moment it exists.

Visibility, not blame. We show both of you everything for one reason: so when something pops into your head, you can see it's already owned and let it go. And when a week feels heavy for one of you, a quick check-in lets the other take some of the load. A shared system, never a scoreboard.

Anticipation, not just reminders. Reminders keep the day on track, and Divly has them. But a reminder only works for a task someone already thought of. Ask Divly adds what reminders can't: it thinks ahead, doing the research, proposing the plan, and building the tasks and lists before the week catches you off guard.

A feeling, not a feature. We don't sell guilt, and we don't keep score. We sell what a well-run home actually buys you: time with the people you love, and wins worth celebrating together.

What is Divly?

Divly is a household management app for couples and families. It combines tasks, lists and habit tracking, synced in real time between partners, with one defining rule: every task has one clear owner, so nothing lives in limbo and nothing lives in just one person's head. Daily progress rings show at a glance how the household or each person is doing, and one view holds today, the plan ahead, and your synced external calendars. Ask Divly, an AI helper powered by Claude, turns a request like "plan the weekend" or "plan this week's dinners" into ready-to-add tasks, lists and grocery lists. Divly is sold per household, not per person, with a 7-day free trial on iOS and Android.

The problem: the mental load

Running a home is a full-time job nobody signed up for. Noticing the fridge is empty. Remembering the school form. Anticipating that allergy season is coming and the prescription needs refilling. These tasks don't show up on anyone's calendar. They live in someone's head, and work that lives in a head can't be shared, only carried.

Productivity apps never solved this because they're built for individuals: your list, your reminders, your account. The household work stayed invisible, and the person who managed it stayed the manager.

Our answer

Divly moves the household out of one person's head and into one shared system both of you see. Every task has an owner, so when something pops into your head, you can see it's already handled and let it go. Ask Divly takes on the heaviest part of the load: it does the research, proposes the plan, and builds the tasks and lists for you. And when a week feels heavy for one of you, a quick check-in lets the other take some of the load.

The result we care about is not more productivity. It's fewer dropped balls, a lighter head, and more evenings that feel like living together instead of managing together. A home that runs like a team, and two people with their weekends back.

Less managing. More living.
