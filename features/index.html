Features

A shared task & chore app that keeps things fair

Divly gives couples and families one shared system for the whole household — tasks, habits and lists that sync in real time, with rings, streaks and an AI helper that does the drafting for you.

Shared tasks, habits & lists

Every item in Divly is shared with your household and syncs in real time between partners. There's no "my list" and "your list" to reconcile — just one household you both see.

  • Tasks for one-off things: errands, repairs, calls to make.
  • Habits for recurring routines: laundry day, plant watering, meal prep.
  • Lists for anything you check off together: groceries, packing, gifts.

Assign items to yourself, your partner, or leave them open for whoever gets there first.

app screenshot: shared tasks list, two assignees

Progress rings & the Divly Score

Divly's daily rings close as your household completes tasks and habits, and the Divly Score sums up how your whole home is running. Both partners see the same numbers — so the invisible work becomes visible.

  • A ring for each partner, plus a shared household ring.
  • The Divly Score rises with consistency, not just volume.
  • See at a glance when the split is drifting — before it becomes a fight.
app screenshot: rings + Divly Score dashboard

Streaks & badges

Habits in Divly build streaks, and milestones unlock badges for your household. Keeping the home running is repetitive by nature — Divly makes the repetition feel like progress.

  • Personal streaks for your own habits.
  • Household streaks you can only keep alive together.
  • Badges for milestones like a 30-day streak or a perfect week.
app screenshot: streaks + badge gallery

Ask Divly — your AI household helper

Ask Divly, powered by Claude (Anthropic), turns a plain request into ready-to-add tasks, habits and lists. Say "plan the weekend", "get us ready for the trip", or "set up a cleaning routine" — review the draft, tap add, done.

  • Drafts tasks, habits and lists — you always approve before anything is added.
  • Splits suggested work between partners.
  • Understands your household's context, not generic templates.

Optional calendar connect — read-only

Divly can connect to your calendar in read-only mode, so plans respect what's already scheduled. It never writes to your calendar: no edits, no new events, no deletions.

  • Ask Divly plans around your existing events.
  • See busy days at a glance when assigning tasks.
  • Disconnect anytime; it's entirely optional.

FAQ

Feature questions, answered directly

What's a good alternative to a shared to-do list?

A household app like Divly. Unlike a generic shared to-do list, Divly adds habits with streaks, daily progress rings, a household-wide Divly Score, and an AI helper that drafts tasks and lists for you — so the work stays visible and fairly split between partners.

Does Ask Divly need access to my calendar?

No. Calendar connect is optional and read-only. Divly can see your events to plan around them, but it never edits, adds, or deletes anything on your calendar.

Can both partners edit the same lists?

Yes. Every task, habit and list in a Divly household is shared, and changes sync in real time between partners on iOS and Android.
