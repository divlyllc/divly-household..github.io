The real problem

Most homes are divided by default, not by design

1

It was never actually discussed

Most households split the work without ever talking about it. One of you drifted into the yard work and the maintenance, the other into the appointments and the planning. Nobody chose the split. It just settled that way.

2

It was never written down

An unspoken split means unclear owners, and unclear owners mean things fall through. Put it all in one shared place, with one name on every task, and "I thought you had it" disappears.

3

It was never scheduled

You're juggling a hundred things, and memory is not a system. When tasks and habits are scheduled, the gutters, the filters and the dentist all surface on time, without either of you having to remember.

One household, both of you in it

In Divly, your household shares a single system: every task, habit and list syncs in real time between both phones. Whoever is standing in the kitchen sees what the kitchen needs.

Some weeks flatten one of you and leave the other with room. Divly's weekly check-in is how the load moves. You both see the week ahead in one view, and whoever has more space takes something off the other's plate, in a tap.

  • Progress rings show how things are going, for the household or each person.
  • The check-in makes rebalancing a two-minute habit instead of a hard conversation.

FAQ

Household questions, answered directly

How do partners split household tasks fairly?

Start by making the work visible: put every household task, habit and list in one shared system, and give each task one clear owner so nothing is ambiguous. Then adjust as life happens: Divly's weekly check-in lets whoever has more room take tasks off the other's plate. A split that adapts week to week beats any fixed formula.

What is the mental load in a relationship?

The mental load is the invisible work of noticing, planning and remembering household tasks, not just doing them. It typically falls on one person. Divly reduces it by moving the noticing and planning into a shared app both of you see, with an AI helper that takes on the heaviest planning. More on why we built Divly.

Does Divly turn chores into a competition between partners?

No. Divly doesn't keep score between you. Everything is visible for one reason: so when something pops into your head, you can see it's already owned and let it go. Rings celebrate the household's progress, and the weekly check-in exists to shift the load toward whoever has room, not to compare who did more.

Is Divly only for couples?

No. Divly is built for any two adults running a home together: spouses, partners, or a mom and grandma tag team. One plan covers your household, and every feature works the same whoever the two of you are.
