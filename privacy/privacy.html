Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

This summary explains what Divly stores and why, in plain language. It applies to the Divly app and the household account you share with your partner. (Testing-phase draft — final wording will be confirmed before public launch.)

What we store

Your account basics (name, email, sign-in provider), your household and its members, and the content you create — tasks, habits, lists, check-ins, and Ask Divly conversations. If you connect a calendar, we mirror event titles and times to power calendar features. We also store your device timezone so daily progress and limits follow your local day.

Where it lives

Your data is stored with Supabase (hosted in the United States), encrypted in transit and at rest. Access is scoped to your household — other households can never read your data.

AI features

When you use Ask Divly, your messages and a compact household summary are sent to Anthropic (the maker of Claude) to generate the reply. During the testing phase we also keep an operational log of each AI call — sizes, token counts, and cost — but never the content of your messages. AI suggestions are ideas, not professional advice.

What we never do

We do not sell your data, show ads, or share household content with third parties beyond the services above that make the app work.

Deleting your data

The household owner can delete the account from Profile → Delete account. Deletion is scheduled immediately and all household data is permanently purged after 30 days (the window lets you change your mind by signing back in and restoring).

Questions

Email privacy@divly.co and a human will get back to you.
